Layer 1 is the dominant bottleneck вЂ” and it's invisible
The VWAP band check at the end of L1 has no log line at all вЂ” it just returns None. Out of 560 scans, only 15 generated a loggable L1 signal. The rest died quietly for one of these reasons (all DEBUG or silent):

The VWAP_BAND_PCT=0.0075 means price must be within 0.75% of VWAP. On a trending day like today, most stocks are either well above or well below VWAP for long stretches вЂ” within 0.75% is a narrow window that only opens briefly.

Layer 3 is the second bottleneck
L3 requires a 1-min candle that is simultaneously green (close > open) AND rising (close > prev close). Both conditions must be true. This is a strict double requirement вЂ” a flat or mildly red candle kills an otherwise valid setup even when the stock is clearly trending. 9 out of 15 signals got killed here.

Where to focus, in order of impact:
1. VWAP band вЂ” highest leverage, safest change
Going back from 0.75% toward the old 0.01 (1.0%) would roughly 2Г— the L1 pass rate. The setup quality doesn't meaningfully degrade вЂ” you're still requiring a VWAP pullback, just with a slightly more forgiving window. On a $200 stock, 0.75% is В±$1.50; 1.0% is В±$2.00. Either is still a "near VWAP" entry.
2. L3 momentum вЂ” second highest impact
Drop the double requirement: keep "rising close > prev close" but remove the "green candle (close > open)" requirement. A 1-min bar can be slightly red (close just below open) while still closing higher than the previous bar вЂ” that's still upward momentum. The current logic sometimes fires one scan cycle late, after the momentum candle has already passed, which means missed entries.
3. Leave L5 alone
The regime gate is working correctly. MRVL and SOFI CALL signals while SPY was bearish should be blocked вЂ” those would likely have been bad trades today.
4. L4/L6 are not the problem
Zero blocks today. They're not overfitting or over-filtering вЂ” they're simply not being triggered.
